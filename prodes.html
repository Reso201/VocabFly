Project Description

Overview

  Topic and Motivation

In today’s modern society, technology, particularly smartphones, have become a part of human’s life, including children. Besides making people’s life become more convenience in many aspects like contacting, researching information and so on, it also brings back. As Penelope Leach – A British psychologist specializes in child development research and writing on parenting topics said that, “For a small child there is no division between playing and learning; between the things he or she does ‘just for fun’ and things that are ‘educational.’ The child learns while living and any part of living that is enjoyable is also play.” [1]. So instead of keeping the phone out of their range, we want to change the way they use their phones for educational purpose by making a video game called VocabFly- inspired by a side-scroller game Flappy bird and Duolingo, an app which most people use to learn English. At main interface, players will choose the topic and level they want to play from easy to hard, then a notification will appear for players to confirm their selection and start the game. The gameplay will be a little harder from the original Flappy bird because we will add a picture on top-left corner and two gates which contain an English word relate to the picture and only one of those are correct, players have three times to choose the answer for every level, so they must try to control the bird to fly into the correct gate as much as they can to finish the level. Otherwise, the game will reset, show them the correct answer and save their highest point as a new record. The only weakness of this game is the limitation of the vocabulary, comparing to learning apps like Duolingo or Quizzlet, we can only provide the basic words to help children get to know about some topics like transportations, animals, household appliances, furniture, and school supplies.

  Landscape

Our team is developing an engine that aims to provide users with the ability to expand their vocabulary in the form of playing video games. In doing so, we have identified a few competitors in the market, including Duolingo and Quizzlet.

However, we believe that our platform has a few special qualities that set us apart from these rivals, including:
- Vocabulary expansion: This feature would be helpful for players to remember synonyms better by choosing the words through the game, and they are going to learn it by heart without feeling like they are studying.
- Stimulate curiosity: Another purpose of making this game is to make people curious about another word. When they choose the correct word and start the next gate, they might want to know about the other one, which is the wrong answer. Then they may do some research on the internet and get a huge amount of vocabulary by doing this.

Detailed Description

  Aims

Ultimate Aims:
Educational Empowerment:

  The primary goal of VocabFly is to combine entertainment with education by providing a gamified platform for users to enhance their vocabulary skills. Users should experience a significant improvement in their vocabulary while enjoying a fun and engaging gaming experience.

Game maintenance and commitment:

   Through playing each stage with difficulties level rises, will result in achievement and form a sense of habit of daily login and progression. In addition, the game will require you to update regularly to fix bugs and add new features with new stages to reinforce the consistency of logging in the app, create addicting nature to learn more vocabulary through gaming.

List of Smaller Aims:
Appealing display:

  Design an easy and childish theme interface to ensure the attention of users within range of age to approach, increasing positive energy for user to play. Allows comfortable environment and motivation to play without struggling with tutorial guidance.

Accommodative learning program::

  Implement a self-improving learning algorithm that increases the level of difficulties based on the progress made by the users. This triggers the users’ brain whenever approaching any failing stages will find another way to adapt and reconsider the decision already created.

Motivation design:

  Adding features like side stories, achievements, leaderboards, limited event stages to lure user into setting time to play. With this, the user had to play with dedication with each decision made and earn huge rewards for accomplishing higher stages. Opening a community for trials and handmade tutorials for players across the platform to read and follow.

Weekly maintenance::

   Provide a consistency of new stages and challenges throughout weekly content, ensuring a long-term engagement with non-stop exciting contents to play with.

Offline Mode:

  Allow app to be playable without using continuous internet connection, easy to access at any time and location without interruption. Users can save stages and collect data information when switching tabs and will not stagger the app performance.

Different device compatibility:

  Devices such as iOS and android should be able to download VocabFly without having trouble switching files and maintain original progress.

Easy going tutorials:

  Delivery easy instructions for users to capture most of the content of the game and how it functions, explain details how to make achievements as well as educational lessons behind each stage.

Progress tracking:

  Implement a progress monitoring system where each lesson is recorded and vocabulary learned is marked as achievement, setting an archive for self-improvement.

Interactive social platform:

  Open an online forum and platform for users to share knowledge, progress, and achievements to bond more friends. Create multiplayer challenges to connect people together, promote a healthy and competitive environment.

Feedback:

  Including a feedback mechanism for changes modification according to user’s wishes, this will be based on the majority for continuous improvement and attract more users to play.

Region content:

  Spreading the app through internet synonymous to different languages apply for different users across the region to play, players will find it comfortable to their preference through setting.

Daily challenges:

  Create an ongoing event through daily login and mission completion to establish habit of playing every day. A routine of vocabulary checking and motivating to learn more.

Difficulties levels

  To create an adventurous feeling when playing, generate a hard mode stage for users wish to learn more hard-spelling vocabulary to play. Experiencing situations where require brainstorming and tricky puzzle to solve, preventing the game from being too boring and easy going.

Offline / online achievements:

  Promote a limited online achievement to push players to associate and interact more with others, this may apply offline as well for users prefer self-satisfaction.

Custom levels:

  All users create and share their own creation of stages for users across platform to try and have fun as well as brainstorming. Different languages will have translations to broaden the vocabulary, enhancing engagement and create a friendly community to join.

  Testing

To ensure that the app publishes smoothly, we had run different types of testing that fits our product maintenance the most. Due to the majority of engineering students in our group, the testing phase ran perfectly without detecting any malfunction. To each test category, we identify what is most fitting for the application:
Functional testing:
When downloading an app and initiating the game, users tend to find flaws in these steps to decide whether the game is worth playing or not. Therefore, having accurate and smooth-running screen and transaction seamlessly, every function should work precisely as required.
Interference testing:
To predict situations like having to tab out to have a phone call or low on battery shut down, we must test how the app responds to each situation and result in a save data game without overgoing restart of the game.
Website/app testing:
Links that are uploaded to mobile or any devices should be checked carefully without any anomaly added when open, verifying external and internal links are must for checkup. Any cookies relating to the issue should be informed beforehand and make sure encrypted notification when opening a link.
Content testing:
We ensure that grammatically and vocabulary check before publishing, all types of face and images will be verified as valid and making sure no lost data is seen when uploading to the main screen.
Performance testing:
Testing how the app works on screentime loading, time opening until enter sleep mode, function reaction to multiple actions at the time, timeframe fit to the requirement, accessibility on different type of devices. Then evaluate overall performance based on data from user’s logging in.

  Risks

Risks encountered are unavoidable elements in any kind of event which can be managed but not wholly eliminated in reality. Any spontaneous risks can cause unpredictable damage to the project, so the solution here is we have to go ahead with the risks proactively and carefully analyze and plan to minimize the possible damage as best we can. Since then, risk management has been the crucial part of the project's process; we use the risk matrix tool to address them based on the potential effects (S) of risks, and the probability of occurrence (P) of it will be graded from one to three along, meaning the lowest to the highest point in both scales. By multiplying those two, the ranking point will be between one and nine to determine the level of risk impact, which will be our main criteria for the decision-making process.

Through careful discussion, we come up with three main risk categories: operational risk, technical, end user, and in-external issues. A brief summary, preventive solutions or reactive actions, and a ranking of the dangers that we have covered will be included in each section.

Technical risks


Since our main product is an app, the technical issues mainly involve application performance and quality of maintenance.
- Damaged computers (PxS=3)
Numerous factors, including poor maintenance, out-of-date or incompatible parts, etc., could have caused this incident. If this happens, we will organize a technical team to fix the computers; if not, we will buy a new one at a reasonable cost. In order to keep the workflow operating at a reduced capacity during this time, we will use a different computer as the substitute plan.
- Corrupted files (PxS=2):
In the case that this happens, our technical staff will look into the files and determine whether they should be deleted, fixed, or recovered. Suppose this file prevents us from conducting business; in that case, we will either use our backup copy or temporarily stop any important but superfluous parts until the problem is resolved. Since most corrupted files can be recovered and revised, we have given this event a severity rating of 2.

End-users issues


The risks from this section are mainly relevant to user experience, which we were dedicated to refining at our best to ensure the quality of our products so that we can grade most of them with such a low score based on various preventive plans to avoid damage.
- Poor documented (PxS=2)
This event might happen when the instructions of the game are not clear enough for such a young-age target customer, which leads to confusion when using it. If this event happens, we will proactively discuss and review our content to fix it as soon as possible.
- Ineffective user interface (PxS=3)
Suppose there is any feedback about the user-friendliness of our app's interface, such as some unnecessary features or some word suggestions that are confusing; in that case, we will gather the design team and review our code to enhance its quality. We grade this problem as this low because we will work hard on the building and testing phase to maximize our product as best as possible before releasing it.
- Ineffective education outcome (PxS=6)
This event might happen when we keep track of and analyze our consumer' learning progress and notice the recession in their performance; we have to look into our game's algorithm and seek a more effective solution so the word suggestion can positively impact education performance.

Crisis – Internal vs External issues


- Conflicts (PxS=6)
Any teamwork has to involve conflicts due to the dissent among members; however, based on how serious the problem is, we will have a suitable solution to settle it. As long as it is controllable, we will not prioritize taking legal action against each other; if not, we must deal with it by abiding by the law.
- Health problems and Accidents: (PxS=4)
If any team member has poor health, we will prioritize their condition and assign them sick leave prescribed by trustworthy dispensaries. Other people will join to cover the left person's job and try to maintain the operation. In such unexpected events, we all have to offer up our own time to work extra to fulfil the emergency tasks. We shall search for a recruit or another party to handle the task if there is someone who has an accident because the recuperation process could take many months. If the recruit does well, they will be allowed to stay.
- External issues:
These issues, which originate from purely external causes such as nature, other people, the government, and society, are the most unexpected of all. Risks from external hazards include storms, floods, earthquakes, and other natural calamities that can catch us off guard at any time. When this happens, two backup copies will be made and kept safe, and only the project manager and one key member are going to have access to them. This solution can make sure our core members, who are expected to be responsible and trustworthy, are the ones who hold the power to recreate the project.
